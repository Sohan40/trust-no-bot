# Product Brief: Trust No Bot

## One-line pitch

Trust No Bot is a web game where you play Mafia-style social deduction against AI characters and try to identify the hidden opponent.

## Core hook

> Can you catch the hidden AI opponent?

The user is not just playing a chat game. They are reading character behavior, asking questions, noticing contradictions, and making a risky vote.

## Target user

Initial target users:

- people who enjoy social deduction games
- students and friend groups who understand Mafia, Werewolf, or Among Us
- people curious about AI characters
- users who enjoy dramatic chat-based games

Do not aim at hardcore competitive gamers first. Build for casual replayability.

## Product feel

The product should feel like:

- a suspicious group chat
- a Mafia/Werewolf game night
- Among Us voting drama
- AI NPCs with hidden roles
- a replayable mini-game, not a chatbot demo

## Main MVP

The first version is:

- one human player
- six AI players
- one hidden Mafia role
- one Detective
- one Doctor
- four Villagers
- day/night phases
- AI discussion
- human questioning
- voting
- result reveal

## What makes it fun

The fun comes from:

1. AI characters having distinct personalities.
2. Innocent characters sometimes sounding suspicious.
3. The user forming theories.
4. Characters remembering accusations and defenses.
5. Vote shifts creating tension.
6. Post-game reveal explaining the key turning points.
7. Shareable results like: "I trusted the wrong bot."

## Differentiation

There are existing social deduction games and text-based Mafia games. Trust No Bot should differentiate through:

- AI personalities
- hidden-role reasoning
- single-player availability anytime
- post-game analysis
- daily challenge seeds
- social theme packs later
- shareable result cards

## MVP success criteria

The MVP is working only if users want to play again.

Track:

- games started
- games completed
- replay rate
- average game length
- share clicks
- user win rate
- mode chosen
- points where users drop off

## Product truth

If the AI dialogue is generic, this product will fail.

The first priority after core rules is not payments, auth, or multiplayer. It is making the AI characters feel distinct and consistent.

## Positioning

Use:

- "Can you catch the hidden AI opponent?"
- "Play Mafia against AI characters. Trust no bot."
- "One of them has a secret role. Can you survive the vote?"

Avoid:

- "AI chatbot game"
- "LLM-based social deduction platform"
- "AI companion Mafia"

## Launch wedge

Launch with Classic Mode and a Daily Challenge.

Daily challenge is powerful because everyone can compare the same seed:

- same characters
- same hidden roles
- same starting setup
- different user questions and votes

## Long-term vision

Phase-wise expansion:

1. Classic single-player
2. AI memory and stronger personalities
3. Detective Mode
4. Mafia Mode where user is the hidden role
5. Chaos roles
6. Daily challenge and share cards
7. Persistence and analytics
8. Premium mode packs
9. Multiplayer foundation
10. Real-time multiplayer only after validation
